About Nicole Sumner

Multi-arts educator, cultural worker, collaborative artist — and Seattle's first grand slam poetry champion.

Nicole hails from Italy, NYC and SF, and has taught pre-K through graduate students in India, Nepal, Alaska, New York, Washington and California.

She is an interdisciplinary educator, program developer, and performing/visual arts teacher interested in making thinking visible, in student-inspired, rhizomatic learning, and in improvisation in educational settings. Alongside teaching and consulting on arts integration, performance poetry, music and movement pedagogy, and Reggio Emilia–inspired inquiry, she has directed and performed in children's theaters, a women's prison, the poetry slam circuit, and jazz/performance-art venues across the US and Europe.

Drawing from 25 years of experience in multiple arts — drama, visual art, music, poetry and movement — I work to draw out participants' multiple intelligences and build individual and community competencies, for the sake of the greater work at hand.

I try to teach in cultural humility, mindful of whose shoulders I stand on. I ask myself who I bring into every classroom — the artists, ancestors, and activists I carry with me — and whose land I am standing on. I come to each room having done my best to think from an equity lens, while knowing I don't know everything: the people in front of me may know more, and I invite their feedback.

Nicole holds an MA with honors in interdisciplinary arts education, a BFA in theater, and a K–12 California music teaching credential. She is certified in Orff-Schulwerk and holds one level each of Kodály and Dalcroze. She is the recipient of numerous education grants and performance awards.
