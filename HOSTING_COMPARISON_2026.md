# Website Hosting — what you paid vs. 2026 options

*Goal: a low-cost, low-maintenance home for the Artways Playways site. Prices are USD and approximate as of early 2026 — confirm the exact number at signup, since intro rates and renewal rates differ a lot (that's the main trap).*

---

## What you were actually paying (from the 2025 GreenGeeks chat)

- **Host:** GreenGeeks, *EcoSite Lite 50* plan, billed on a **3-year term**.
- **Renewal rate:** **$12.95/month → $466.20 for 3 years** (about **$155/year**). They offered a one-time 50% renewal discount bringing it to $233.10 for 3 years (~$78/year).
- **Domain** (`nicolesumner.org`): **$19.95/year**, separate.
- **New-customer promo** (if you restart from scratch): EcoSite Lite 25 at **$2.95/month the first year**, then back up to **$12.95/month**.
- **Status now:** you cancelled in 2025; the site shows a "Coming soon" page. You're (or were) still paying the ~$20/year domain renewal to hold the name.

> The key thing the GreenGeeks agent confirmed: the cheap numbers ($2.95/mo) are **first-year intro only**. The real ongoing cost on that style of plan is **~$155/year + $20 domain = ~$175/year**. That's the bar to beat.

**One more wrinkle:** back in 2016 you also had a separate **WordPress.com Premium** plan (a different thing from GreenGeeks, which hosts self-installed WordPress.org). You don't need both. Whatever you choose, you want **one** host, not a tangle of overlapping subscriptions.

---

## The real question first: do you still need WordPress at all?

Your site is small — maybe 10–12 pages, a blog, photo galleries, a contact form. That's the profile where the classic "self-hosted WordPress" setup (which is what GreenGeeks was) is **more machinery than you need**: you have to do your own updates, security, and plugin maintenance (exactly the "sounds like a pain" the WordPress support person described to you in 2016). For a site this size, an all-in-one builder or a simple modern setup is cheaper *and* less work.

So the options below are grouped by **how much you want to touch the machinery.**

---

## Option group A — All-in-one builders (host + design + maintenance bundled; least work)

| Option | ~Annual cost (incl. domain) | Best for | Trade-offs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Squarespace** | ~$200–215/yr (≈$16–23/mo billed yearly; domain often free year 1, then ~$20) | **Recommended.** Beautiful templates, drag-and-drop, galleries + blog built in, nothing to maintain. Strong fit for an arts/portfolio site. | Costs roughly the same as your old GreenGeeks bill, but you get the design tools and zero maintenance for that price. |
| **Wix** | ~$160–230/yr depending on plan | Most flexible drag-and-drop placement (you liked "moving photos by dragging" — Wix is the strongest at that). | Templates can't be swapped after launch; can feel busier. |
| **WordPress.com** (their hosted plans, not self-host) | ~$100–180/yr for a plan that allows a custom domain + no ads | If you want to stay in the WordPress family with none of the self-host upkeep. | Lower tiers are limited; the "real" flexibility tiers cost more. |

## Option group B — Cheap shared hosting (you install/maintain WordPress; most like your old setup)

| Option | ~Annual cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hostinger / Namecheap / DreamHost shared plans** | **~$35–60/yr** intro, **~$100–140/yr** at renewal, + domain | Cheapest *sticker* price, but you're back to doing updates and security yourself. Watch the intro-vs-renewal jump. |
| **Restart GreenGeeks** | ~$35/yr year 1, ~$175/yr after + domain | Only worth it if you specifically want them back; no real advantage over the others. |

## Option group C — Simple/modern, very low cost (a little technical setup, then nearly free)

| Option | ~Annual cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Carrd** | **~$19/yr** + domain (~$15) ≈ **$35/yr total** | If you want something *very* small — basically a rich one-to-few-page site. Probably too limited for your blog + galleries + audience pages. |
| **Static site (Netlify / GitHub Pages) + custom domain** | **Hosting free; ~$15/yr domain only** | Cheapest possible and zero maintenance/security worry, but needs someone comfortable with the setup (this is the kind of thing I could help build directly). No built-in blog editor unless we add one. |

---

## My recommendation

1. **If you want it handled and good-looking with no upkeep → Squarespace.** It costs about what GreenGeeks did (~$200/yr) but replaces *both* the host and the designer you used to pay separately, and you'll never patch a plugin. For an arts consultant's portfolio + blog + Playshop menu, it's the natural fit.

2. **If keeping cost rock-bottom matters most and you don't mind me building it → a static site on Netlify/GitHub Pages.** Then your only bill is the **~$15–20/year domain.** Given your content is now well-organized (the content map + Playshops page are ready), this is very doable, and I can do the build.

3. **Either way: keep the `nicolesumner.org` domain renewing (~$20/yr) so you don't lose the name** while deciding. That's the one bill worth paying even during the pause.

**Rough yearly totals to compare:** old setup ~$175 · Squarespace ~$200 · cheap shared host ~$120 (after intro) · static site ~$20.

> Prices drift and intro deals change — verify the renewal (not just the first-year) number before committing. Everything above reflects early-2026 rates.
